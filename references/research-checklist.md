# Research Checklist

Use this checklist when verifying a diary.

## What to Search

- Weather for the target date and location.
- News or public events mentioned by the user.
- Names of people, companies, products, places, books, films, papers, policies, and dates.
- Claims preceded by uncertainty markers: "好像", "似乎", "应该是", "不确定", "maybe", "I think", "apparently", "not sure".
- Anything that could have changed recently.

## Source Preferences

- Prefer primary sources: official sites, original announcements, weather services, government pages, papers, company pages.
- Use reputable secondary sources for news context when primary sources are unavailable.
- Compare at least two sources for surprising or consequential claims.
- Record URLs in `fact_checks.sources`.

## How to Correct

- Do not silently replace what the writer said, believed, or felt in the diary voice.
- If a factual statement is wrong, preserve the writer's original belief when it matters to the diary, then mention the correction in verification notes.
- If the user was unsure but mostly correct, preserve the user's uncertainty unless the user asked for a more certain factual rewrite.
- If the evidence is unresolved, keep the uncertainty visible.
- Do not turn the diary into a report; facts support the narrative.

## Weather

Use the target date, location, and timezone. Include a human-friendly summary, temperature range, and notable rain/wind/air-quality details if they affected the day.

## Citations

The final diary may keep citations out of the prose, but the final response should include important sources used. In the local Markdown file, include a concise verification section only when it improves trust or documents a meaningful correction.
