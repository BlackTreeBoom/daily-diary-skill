# Writing Fidelity

Use this reference before drafting diary prose.

## Core Rule

The diary belongs to the writer. Preserve the writer's thoughts, wording, biases, emotions, point of view, priorities, and self-understanding. The skill may organize, format, sequence, translate, and fact-check objective claims, but it must not replace the writer's mind with the agent's preferred interpretation.

## Allowed

- Arrange material into a clear timeline.
- Remove obvious duplication and filler that does not change meaning.
- Correct typos or speech-to-text errors when meaning is clear.
- Translate naturally while preserving attitude and stance.
- Add date, weekday, weather, and source context.
- Verify objective facts and record corrections in verification notes.
- Make the prose readable without changing what the writer appears to believe, feel, or value.

## Not Allowed

- Do not sanitize prejudice, anger, resentment, shame, pride, desire, uncertainty, or contradiction.
- Do not make the writer more balanced, generous, mature, optimistic, careful, polite, or neutral than the source material.
- Do not add motivations, lessons, apologies, gratitude, empathy, or moral framing that the writer did not express.
- Do not replace first-person judgments with detached analysis.
- Do not convert subjective statements into objective claims.
- Do not erase hedges such as "I think", "maybe", "seems", "好像", "似乎", or "不确定" unless the writer explicitly wants certainty and the fact check supports it.

## Fact Checking

Separate objective corrections from diary voice:

- Diary voice: preserve what the writer said or believed at the time.
- Verification notes: state what was checked, what evidence showed, and whether the original claim was verified, corrected, or unresolved.

Example:

```text
Diary voice: I kept thinking the meeting was with the Series B founder, and that uncertainty colored the conversation.

Verification Notes: corrected - the company announced its Series A in 2025; no Series B announcement was found in the checked sources.
```

Do not silently rewrite the diary voice to say "the Series A founder" if the writer's mistaken belief mattered to the day's experience.

## Translation

When translating, preserve stance before elegance. A blunt source should remain blunt; an uncertain source should remain uncertain; a biased source should remain biased. Improve readability only within those boundaries.
